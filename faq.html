What is Elliott Wave Theory?
Elliott Wave Theory is a method of technical analysis developed by Ralph Nelson Elliott in the 1930s. It identifies recurring price patterns based on crowd psychology and market sentiment. The theory suggests that markets move in 5-wave impulses and 3-wave corrections.
How to identify Wave 3 breakout in Nifty?
Wave 3 is identified when price breaks above Wave 1 high with strong volume and momentum. It is never the shortest wave and often extends to 161.8% of Wave 1. Key signs include high volume, strong RSI, and MACD above zero line.
What are the 3 absolute rules of Elliott Wave?
1. Wave 2 cannot retrace more than 100% of Wave 1. 2. Wave 3 is never the shortest wave. 3. Wave 4 cannot enter the price territory of Wave 1. These rules cannot be broken—if they are, the wave count is invalid.
How to use Fibonacci with Elliott Wave?
Common retracement levels are 38.2%, 50%, and 61.8%. Extension levels like 100%, 161.8%, and 261.8% are used for Wave 3 targets. Fibonacci helps confirm wave counts and identify entry and exit points.
What is the difference between Wave 2 and Wave 4 correction?
Wave 2 is a sharp correction that retraces 38.2%–61.8% of Wave 1. Wave 4 is a sideways correction that retraces 23.6%–38.2% of Wave 3. Wave 4 is typically more complex and takes more time.
What is Ending Diagonal in Elliott Wave?
An Ending Diagonal is a wedge-shaped pattern that occurs in Wave 5 or Wave C. It signals trend exhaustion and an impending sharp reversal. It is characterized by overlapping waves and converging trendlines.
How to spot Wave 5 exhaustion?
Wave 5 exhaustion is identified by momentum divergence on RSI or MACD. Price makes a new high but indicators fail to confirm. Volume typically declines and the wave often forms an ending diagonal pattern.
Is Elliott Wave Theory reliable for trading?
Elliott Wave Theory is a powerful tool when used correctly. No theory is 100% accurate, but with proper rules, Fibonacci tools, and confluence with other indicators, it can provide a high probability edge in trading.
