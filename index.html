// Elliott Wave — Complete Guide

Theory, Rules,
Guidelines & Patterns

Ralph Nelson Elliott (1871–1948) discovered that stock market prices unfold in specific patterns. These patterns reflect investor psychology and repeat at every scale of trend.

THE COMPLETE 5-3 WAVE CYCLE
// The 3 Absolute Rules
RULE 01 — WAVE 2
Never Retraces Wave 1 Fully
Wave 2 corrects Wave 1 but can NEVER retrace 100% or more of Wave 1. If it does, the wave count is incorrect and must be re-labeled.
RULE 02 — WAVE 3
Never the Shortest Impulse
Wave 3 is never the shortest among Waves 1, 3, and 5. It is almost always the longest and the most powerful with high volume.
RULE 03 — WAVE 4
No Price Overlap with Wave 1
Wave 4 cannot enter the price territory of Wave 1 in a standard impulse. An exception exists only in Diagonal Triangle patterns.
CORRECTIVE — WAVE B
B Never Exceeds Wave 5 High
In a standard ABC correction, Wave B is a counter-trend bounce that retraces 38%–78% of Wave A. It must NOT exceed the origin of Wave A (Wave 5 high in a bull market).
// Guidelines (Not Rules)

Trading Guidelines

🔄
Principle of Alternation
If Wave 2 is sharp (zigzag), Wave 4 will be sideways (flat or triangle) — and vice versa. This helps anticipate the correction type before it forms.
📏
Wave 3 Extension
Wave 3 is most commonly 161.8% of Wave 1. Extended Wave 3s can reach 261.8% or even 423.6% of Wave 1 in strong trending markets.
🎯
Wave 4 Target Zone
Wave 4 typically retraces 23.6%–38.2% of Wave 3. The 4th wave frequently ends near the price territory of the prior Wave 4 of one lower degree.
⚖️
Wave 5 & Equality
Wave 5 is often equal in length to Wave 1, or travels 61.8% of the distance covered by Waves 1 through 3. Watch for momentum divergence at Wave 5 tops.
🌊
Fibonacci Relationships
Elliott Wave and Fibonacci are inseparable. Every wave has a Fibonacci relationship to its adjacent wave — retracements of 38.2%, 50%, 61.8% and extensions of 100%, 161.8%, 261.8%.
// Elliott Wave Patterns

All EW Patterns

Standard Impulse
The classic 5-wave motive pattern driving the primary trend. Waves 1, 3, 5 are impulses; Waves 2, 4 are corrections. Wave 3 is always the strongest.
MOTIVE
Zigzag (5-3-5)
Sharp corrective pattern (5-3-5). Wave B retraces less than 75% of A. Wave C usually equals Wave A. Common in Wave 2 corrections.
CORRECTIVE
Flat (3-3-5)
Sideways correction (3-3-5). Wave B retraces nearly 100% of Wave A. Common in Wave 4. Three sub-types: Regular, Expanded, Running Flat.
CORRECTIVE
Triangle (3-3-3-3-3)
Five 3-wave structures (ABCDE) forming converging trendlines. Contracting, Expanding, Ascending and Descending types. Appears in Wave 4 or Wave B.
TRIANGLE
Ending Diagonal (Wedge)
A wedge-shaped pattern with overlapping waves appearing at the end of Wave 5 or Wave C. Signals trend exhaustion and impending sharp reversal.
REVERSAL
Double Three (W-X-Y)
Two corrective patterns connected by an X wave. Complex correction that takes more time and can mislead traders expecting trend resumption.
